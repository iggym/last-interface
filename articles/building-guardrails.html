06 / COUNTERFACTUAL BRANCHER

Skip orchestration — or build it first?

Forrester and Gartner both call multi-agent orchestration the defining infrastructure priority of 2026 — the same role Kubernetes played for containers. Pick a path.

Week 2 — First agent works fine solo. Feels validated.
Week 6 — Second use case needs a second agent. No shared identity layer, so each one gets bespoke access rules — exactly the pattern behind the 78% of orgs that don't treat agents as identity-bearing entities.
Week 10 — One provider has an outage. Nothing routes around it — single-model architectures are already breaking whole workflows at this stage in 2026.
Week 14 — Now retrofitting orchestration under a live system. This is the exact shape of the "innovation theater" pattern Gartner cites in its 40%-cancellation-by-2027 projection.

Three models, not five hundred facts

Model 1

The gap is the story. Capability is improving fast — the gap between what agents can do and what gets safely shipped is where almost everyone loses.

Model 2

Boring beats ambitious. Narrow, bought, high-volume, governed — beats broad, built, novel, ungoverned — nearly every time in the current data.

Model 3

Infrastructure is a sequencing decision. Orchestration and identity aren't nice-to-haves added later — skipping them is what turns a pilot into permanent purgatory.
