04 · scenario brancher

It finds a $340 billing error. Now what?

Before you pick — which do you think actually builds more long-term trust: fixing it silently, or telling you right away?

The upside — zero cognitive load. This is the exact leverage the "quiet loop" pattern is built for: cross-function agents that see email, calendar and records at once already cut measured busywork by roughly half.
The risk — this is where The 200 OK Lie lives. If the agent's read of "error" was itself wrong, a silent fix means the mistake compounds with nothing to catch it. Silence hides the good outcome and the bad one identically.
Six months later — if it was right every time, you've built maximal trust and never noticed the work. If it was wrong once, you find out from a bank statement, not from the agent — and that single event undoes a lot of accumulated trust at once.
